D-TS: Statistical Foundation
0:30

The discounted statistics in Equations 3-1 and 3-2 exponentially down-weight older observations via gamma-to-the-power-of-t-minus-j. At gamma 0.99, an observation from 100 steps ago retains about 37 percent weight.

The posterior mean mu-hat is discounted reward over discounted count, and variance decreases with more data — giving Thompson Sampling its natural exploration-exploitation balance. Less data means wider posteriors, which means more exploration.

Methodology
D-TS: Operator Selection Policy
0:30

Equation 3-13 integrates three mechanisms in priority order. Case 1: stagnation override — after 10 consecutive non-improving evaluations, force the best operator group. Case 2: epsilon-floor — with probability 0.15, select uniformly at random to prevent arm abandonment. Case 3: standard Thompson Sampling — draw from each arm’s Gaussian posterior, pick the highest sample.

These are complementary: epsilon guarantees coverage, D-T-S provides adaptive selection, and stagnation override escapes convergence traps.

Reward System
0:20

The reward function is piecewise: invalid code yields zero, valid code yields the A-O-C-C fitness clipped to zero–one. We use absolute fitness, not relative improvement, to avoid reward inflation as the population approaches the fitness ceiling.

Experimental Setup
0:30

We follow the BLADE protocol by van Stein et al. on MA-BBOB — 24 noiseless functions in 5 dimensions, with affine combinations preventing structural exploitation. Configuration: 100 LLM evaluations per run, 5 seeds, mu equals 4 maintained parents, lambda equals 8 offspring, initialization size of 8.

Baselines: original LLaMEA and Evolution of Heuristics. The A-O-C-C formula integrates convergence quality across the entire evaluation budget.

Why GA-LLaMEA Outperforms
